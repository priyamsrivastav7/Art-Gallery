Johannes Vermeer, Girl with a Pearl
The painting is a tronie, the Dutch 17th-century description of a "head" that was not meant to be a portrait. It depicts a European girl wearing "exotic dress", an "oriental turban", and what appears to be a very large pearl as an earring.
Vincent van Gogh, The Starry Night
I can see an enclosed square of wheat ... above which, in the morning, I watch the sun rise in all its glory
Gustav Klimt, The Kiss
The couple locked in an intimate embrace against a gold, flat background. The two figures are situated at the edge of a patch of flowery meadow that ends under the woman's exposed feet
Norman Rockwell, Boy and girl gazing at the moon
I know now that all I need in my work is at hand The commonplaces of America are to me the richest subjects in art. Boys batting flies on vacant lots; little girls playing jacks on the front steps; old men plodding home at twilight, umbrellas in hand all of these things arouse feeling in me
